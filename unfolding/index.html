Research context

Problem space

A tesseract is the 4-dimensional analogue of the cube. Each unfolding places its eight cubic cells in 3D while preserving adjacency.

Symmetry signal

The red models mark the 22 unfoldings with 90-degree rotational symmetry, making higher-order structure easier to spot across the full set.

Viewer behavior

Each tile uses its own orbit controls, so you can inspect individual unfoldings while the catalog continues to animate as a whole.
